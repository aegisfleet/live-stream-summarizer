📝 Overview

A collaboration stream between Juufuutei Raden and the Yametane Museum of Art. It started with a deep dive into Higashiyama Kaii's concluding work of the "Kyōraku Shiki Series," followed by the introduction of various Japanese paintings, including Yamaguchi Akira's massive "Tokyo Zu" (Tokyo Map) mixing past and present, Hayami Gyoshū's Italian sketches, Okuda Gensō's vivid autumn leaves, Yokoyama Taikan's waterfall expressed through ink shades, and powerful works by Takeuchi Seihō and Kawabata Ryūshi. In the final stage, she provided a detailed food review of Japanese confectionery linked to the exhibition and introduced a "nuigurumi photo spot" (stuffed toy photo spot) within the museum. The stream concluded with a passionate discussion about the unique appeal of viewing Nihonga (Japanese painting) firsthand, such as the dedicated lighting and the beauty of "negative space" (yohaku), urging viewers to visit the exhibition.
