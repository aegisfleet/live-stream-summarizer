This is a casual stream where Juufuutei Raden talks about her recent fluorite mining experience and subsequent sightseeing in Nagoya. She reflects on a packed agenda, including a fantastical "Milky Way" experience at the mining site, the scientific principles behind fluorite's luminescence, and an unexpected overnight stay in Nagoya due to a Shinkansen train suspension. At the Nagoya City Science Museum, she was deeply impressed by the abundance of hands-on exhibits, such as a 3D periodic table, cutting-edge science displays, metal fatigue experiments, and a tornado generator. In particular, she enjoyed the best astronomical observation experience in comfortable reclining seats at the optical planetarium, which recreates a starry sky close to reality. While expressing surprise at the sheer scale of the entire facility, she conveys how she enjoyed many exhibits and felt refreshed both mentally and physically. This stream is recommended not only for those interested in geology and minerals but also for anyone who wants to hear unique stories about science museum experiences.
